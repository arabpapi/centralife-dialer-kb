# Manager QA and Scorecards

## How This Works

- Every call scored. No exceptions. If it's not scored, it didn't happen.
- Scoring is diagnostic — it tells you where the rep broke down and what to coach.
- Two tools: the **Call Scorecard** (per-call grading) and the **Weekly QA Summary** (pattern detection).
- Manager listens to minimum 3 calls per rep per week. More during ramp.

---

## Call Scorecard — Full Evaluation

Score each section 1–5. Anchor definitions below.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Missing entirely or fundamentally broken |
| 2 | Attempted but ineffective — client didn't respond |
| 3 | Functional — hit the stage but lacked depth or specificity |
| 4 | Strong — specific, controlled, created forward movement |
| 5 | Elite — natural, precise, created a visible shift in the client's position |

---

### Section 1: Hypothesis Opener (5 points)

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Led with a hypothesis about the client's situation (not a generic intro) | /5 |
| Established credibility within the first 20 seconds | /5 |
| Created curiosity — client engaged rather than deflected | /5 |

**Red flags:** "Hi, I'm calling about your life insurance inquiry." Generic. No hypothesis. No credibility signal.

**What 5 looks like:** "I work with homeowners in your area, and what we're consistently finding is a 3–5x gap between what families carry and what the math actually requires. I'd like to walk you through what I'm seeing."

---

### Section 2: Frame Control and Discovery Transition (5 points)

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Maintained control of the conversation — didn't fold on frame breaks | /5 |
| Transitioned to discovery as a statement, not a request | /5 |
| No permission language ("mind if I ask," "would it be okay," "can I ask you") | /5 |

**Red flags:** "Would it be okay if I asked you a few questions?" "Do you have time for this?" "I don't want to take too much of your time."

**What 5 looks like:** "Let me walk through your situation — it takes about 10 minutes — so I can see what we're working with."

---

### Section 3: IPAD Discovery Depth (5 points)

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Completed all four IPAD sections (Income, Protection, Assets/Debts, Medical) | /5 |
| Went 3 levels deep on critical data points | /5 |
| Stated the uncovered number at the end of each section | /5 |
| Medical section delivered with neutral tone — no judgment on disclosures | /5 |

**Red flags:** Skipping assets. Accepting "I have savings" without quantifying. Rushing the medical section. Not calculating the gap number.

**What 5 looks like:** "So we've got take-home at $4,800, mortgage at $1,900, total monthly debts at $740, savings covers about 3 months, and current coverage is $100K through work. Let me put this together."

---

### Section 4: Rational Drowning (5 points)

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Used the client's specific numbers (not generic examples) | /5 |
| Quantified the gap clearly — stated the dollar amount | /5 |
| Created tension — didn't immediately resolve it with a solution | /5 |

**Red flags:** "Most people have a gap." Generic. No numbers. No pause.

**What 5 looks like:** "Here's where you are: income replacement for 10 years is $576K. Mortgage balance is $220K. Debts are $48K. Education for two kids, conservatively, $160K. That's just over $1M in liability against $100K in coverage from work that doesn't follow you. The gap is $900K."

---

### Section 5: Emotional Impact and Future-Self Framing (5 points)

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Bridged from numbers to human impact using client's words/names | /5 |
| Created urgency without manipulation — grounded in their reality | /5 |
| Used future-self language — what the client is building, protecting, leaving behind | /5 |

**Red flags:** Staying purely in numbers. No emotional bridge. No reference to spouse, children, or legacy. Or going too far — manipulative, fear-mongering tone.

**What 5 looks like:** "You've spent 15 years building this — the house, the career, the retirement runway. For [spouse name], one event resets all of that. Not just financially — structurally. The kids' school. The mortgage. The trajectory you've been working toward. That's what we're solving."

---

### Section 6: Reframe Quality (5 points)

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Delivered a reframe that taught the client something new | /5 |
| Client's response indicated a perspective shift ("I never thought of it that way") | /5 |
| Didn't argue or rebut — repositioned the frame | /5 |

**Red flags:** "No, that's not right" — arguing. "But you really need this" — begging. Reframe that confirms the client's existing belief instead of shifting it.

**What 5 looks like:** "Your employer coverage is 2x salary — that's about $120K. The real need is $900K. And that $120K disappears if you change jobs. You're building your family's safety net on infrastructure you don't control."

---

### Section 7: Transformation Selling (5 points)

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Moved the conversation beyond protection into wealth/accumulation/legacy | /5 |
| Positioned value of living benefits, tax-advantaged growth, or generational transfer | /5 |
| Delivered transformation bridge at the right moment (after gap is established, not before) | /5 |
| Compliance-safe — no guaranteed returns, no "better than your 401k," proper positioning | /5 |

**Red flags:** Jumping to IUL pitch before establishing the protection gap. Guaranteeing returns. Bashing the client's existing investments. Never mentioning transformation at all.

**What 5 looks like:** "That's the protection side. Here's what a properly designed policy also does: it builds tax-advantaged cash value you can access through policy loans, it includes living benefits if your health changes, and the death benefit transfers income-tax-free to the next generation. Protection is the floor — this builds above it."

---

### Section 8: Option Presentation (5 points)

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Presented two options (not one, not three) | /5 |
| Option A (full architecture) presented first with conviction | /5 |
| Trade-offs between options were clear | /5 |
| Client chose — wasn't pushed into one | /5 |

**Red flags:** One option only. Leading with the cheap option. Three options that create paralysis. "Which one do you want?" without clear framing of trade-offs.

---

### Section 9: Close Execution (5 points)

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Close was a powerful request, not a permission ask | /5 |
| Application flow was smooth — no dead air, no fumbling | /5 |
| Handled hesitation without folding | /5 |
| Post-close: referral asked + review requested | /5 |

**Red flags:** "So, what do you think?" "Would you like to go ahead?" "Does that work for you?" Dead air during the application. No referral ask.

**What 5 looks like:** "Let's get this locked in while your rate is at this level. I need a few things from you to get started." Then seamless demographics. Then: "Who else in your family or circle should be having this conversation?"

---

### Section 10: Product Knowledge Depth (5 points)

| Criteria | Score |
|----------|-------|
| Positioned IUL/cash value accurately when relevant | /5 |
| Explained living benefits clearly (chronic, critical, terminal triggers) | /5 |
| Handled carrier/product questions with confidence | /5 |
| Didn't overstate guarantees or misrepresent policy mechanics | /5 |

**Red flags:** "This is basically a savings account." "You're guaranteed X%." Unable to explain how index crediting works. Can't name which carriers offer living benefit riders.

### Scoring Bands

| Band | Range | Action |
|------|-------|--------|
| Elite | 150–175 | Peer coaching candidate. Minimal intervention. |
| Strong | 120–149 | Sharpen 1–2 sections. Targeted drill work. |
| Developing | 90–119 | Structured coaching plan. Daily review. |
| Critical | Below 90 | Daily ride-along. Manager-involved on calls. |

### Pattern Detection Questions
1. Which section scores below 3 for more than one rep? → **That's a team training gap. Address in the daily session.**
2. Which rep has the largest gap between their strongest and weakest section? → **Imbalanced development. Coach the gap.**
3. Is anyone consistently losing it at the same stage? → **Structural issue. Drill that stage relentlessly.**
4. Are transformation selling scores improving week over week? → **If not, the reps aren't moving past protection-only conversations.**
5. Are product knowledge scores keeping pace with selling skills? → **Skills without knowledge = confident incompetence.**

## Stop Tolerating List

These are not coaching items. They're non-negotiables.

| Behavior | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Permission language on calls ("mind if I ask," "would it be okay") | Immediate correction. Play the clip. Coach the replacement. |
| Skipping IPAD sections | Call doesn't count. Doesn't go on the board. Redo. |
| No Rational Drowning summary | Rep didn't quantify. The call lacked structure. Coach immediately. |
| Generic reframes ("most people need insurance") | Teaching moment. Replay. Show them what a real reframe sounds like. |
| Guarantee language ("you're guaranteed 7%") | Compliance issue. Address same day. Document. |
| No transformation bridge in a premium-eligible conversation | Missed revenue. The client deserved to see the full picture and didn't. |
| Weak close ("So, what do you think?") | Play it back. "Was that a request or a question? Because the client heard a question." |
| No referral ask | Money left on the table every single call. Non-negotiable. |
| No post-call CRM update | Pipeline blind spot. 24-hour rule: if it's not in the CRM, it didn't happen. |
