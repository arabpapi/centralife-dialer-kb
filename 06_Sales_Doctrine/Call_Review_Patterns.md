# Live Call Review and Leader Corrections

## How This Works

- Weekly live call review. Minimum 60 minutes. Non-negotiable.
- Pull 3–5 calls: at least 1 strong, at least 1 broken, at least 1 from a developing rep.
- The group diagnoses. The leader corrects. Nobody watches passively.
- Use the Call Review Rubric below to anchor every discussion.

---

## Call Review Rubric

Score each dimension 1–5: **1** = missing/broken, **3** = functional, **5** = elite.

### Dimension 1: Opening Authority

| Criteria | What to Listen For |
|----------|-------------------|
| Hypothesis opener — specific to the client's profile | Led with insight, not a generic intro |
| Credibility established in under 20 seconds | Mentioned independent positioning, carrier count, or experience |
| Curiosity created — client engaged, asked a question, or agreed to continue | Client leaned in rather than deflecting |
| Zero permission language in the first 60 seconds | No "mind if I ask," "is now a good time?", "would it be okay" |

**Elite marker:** The client says "Okay, tell me more" or asks a question within the first 30 seconds.

---

### Dimension 2: Frame Control

| Criteria | What to Listen For |
|----------|-------------------|
| Maintained control when the client tried to redirect | "What's this going to cost?" was handled, not answered prematurely |
| Discovery transitioned as a statement, not a request | "Let me walk through your situation" — not "Can I ask you some questions?" |
| Pacing and silence used intentionally | Paused after key statements. Didn't rush to fill silence. |
| Recovered from frame breaks without losing position | Client pushed back; rep redirected and continued |

**Elite marker:** Client follows the rep's lead for the entire discovery without attempting to redirect more than once.

---

### Dimension 3: Discovery Depth

| Criteria | What to Listen For |
|----------|-------------------|
| All IPAD sections completed | Income, Protection, Assets/Debts, Medical — nothing skipped |
| Went 3+ levels deep on critical data points | Didn't accept "I have savings" — drilled into amount, months of coverage, structure |
| Stated the uncovered number before moving on | "So your total liability is approximately $X against $Y in coverage" |
| Medical section delivered neutrally | No tone change on tobacco, DUI, chronic condition disclosures |
| Questions had clear purpose — no filler | Every question earned data or created movement |

**Elite marker:** Rep states the complete gap number — income replacement + debts + mortgage + education — with the client's specific figures, and the client confirms or corrects the math.

---

### Dimension 4: Rational Drowning and Emotional Impact

| Criteria | What to Listen For |
|----------|-------------------|
| Used client's specific numbers, not industry averages | "$576K in income replacement" — not "most families need $500K" |
| Gap was quantified and stated clearly | The dollar amount was said out loud |
| Tension was created and held — rep didn't immediately pivot to a solution | Pause after the gap statement. Let the math settle. |
| Emotional bridge used the client's names and situation | Referenced spouse, children, specific goals |
| Future-self framing present | What the client is building, protecting, or leaving — not just what they'll lose |

**Elite marker:** The client goes quiet after the Rational Drowning summary. That silence is the reframe working.

---

### Dimension 5: Reframe Quality

| Criteria | What to Listen For |
|----------|-------------------|
| Reframe taught something new | Client's response: "I never thought of it that way" or equivalent |
| Didn't argue or rebut — repositioned | No "but you really need this" — instead, new information that shifts the frame |
| Used specific data, not generic claims | "Your employer coverage is $120K against a $900K need" — not "most people are underinsured" |
| Follow-up question deepened the shift | The reframe opened a door; the follow-up walked through it |

**Elite marker:** The client changes their position unprompted. They move toward the solution without being pushed.

---

### Dimension 6: Transformation Selling

| Criteria | What to Listen For |
|----------|-------------------|
| Conversation moved beyond protection into wealth/accumulation/legacy | Mentioned tax-advantaged growth, living benefits, or generational transfer |
| Transformation bridge delivered after the protection gap — not before | Timing was right. The client understood the gap before hearing about upside. |
| Compliance-safe positioning | No "guaranteed returns," no "better than your 401k," hedged appropriately |
| Client engaged with the transformation concept | Asked questions about cash value, living benefits, or how policies build value |

**Elite marker:** The client says something like "I had no idea life insurance could do that" or "so this isn't just a death benefit?"

---

### Dimension 7: Option Presentation and Close

| Criteria | What to Listen For |
|----------|-------------------|
| Two options presented — full architecture first | Option A (protection + accumulation + living benefits) before Option B (coverage-focused) |
| Trade-offs clearly articulated | Client understood what they gain and lose with each option |
| Close was a powerful request — not a permission ask | "Let's get this locked in" — not "so, what do you think?" |
| Application executed smoothly — no dead air, no fumbling | Steady rhythm. Ask → Wait → Confirm → Next. |
| Post-close: referral asked + review requested | "Who else should be having this conversation?" + "A quick Google review goes a long way." |

**Elite marker:** Client chooses Option A without being pushed. The framing made the decision obvious.

## Leader Correction Protocols

### How to Deliver Corrections

1. **Play the clip.** 20–60 seconds maximum. Everyone hears the same thing.
2. **Ask the group:** "What happened here?" Let them diagnose first.
3. **State the correction.** One sentence. "The frame broke here. The recovery should have been [X]."
4. **Demo the correction.** Leader or top rep delivers what it should have sounded like.
5. **Rep re-delivers.** The person who made the call delivers the corrected version. Out loud. Right now.
6. **Move on.** Don't camp on one mistake. Correct and continue.

### Correction Categories

**Category A — Language Corrections**
| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Permission language used | Replace with direct statement. Demo the replacement. |
| Generic opener | Rebuild as hypothesis. Rep re-delivers. |
| Filler questions | Identify the question's intended job. Rebuild with purpose. |
| Weak close | Replace with powerful request. Rep delivers 3 times. |
| Guarantee language or compliance violation | Address immediately. Correct the positioning. Document. |

**Category B — Structure Corrections**
| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| IPAD section skipped | Rep re-runs the skipped section in role-play |
| No Rational Drowning summary | Rep delivers the summary using the call's data |
| No transformation bridge | Rep practices the bridge using the client's profile |
| One option presented instead of two | Rep builds both options from the call's IPAD data |
| Application fumbled | Rep runs demographics drill until smooth |

**Category C — Depth Corrections**
| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Surface-level discovery (accepted first answer) | Rep practices going 3 levels deep on one question |
| No emotional impact — stayed in numbers only | Rep practices the bridge: numbers → names → impact → future |
| Reframe that argued instead of taught | Demo the teaching version. Rep re-delivers until the group agrees it teaches. |
| No future-self or legacy framing | Rep practices one future-self sequence from memory |
| Product knowledge gap during the call | Assign study: carrier comparison, living benefit triggers, IUL mechanics. Review next session. |

## Call Review Session Structure

### 60-Minute Format

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–5 min | Context: which calls are we reviewing and why |
| 5–20 min | Call 1 — strong call. What worked. What's replicable. |
| 20–40 min | Call 2 — broken call. Group diagnoses. Leader corrects. Rep re-delivers. |
| 40–55 min | Call 3 — developing rep. Specific coaching. One correction only. |
| 55–60 min | Each rep states one takeaway + one commitment for the week |

### Rules for the Room

1. **No piling on.** One correction per clip. Don't bury the rep in 6 things to fix.
2. **Specificity only.** "You need to be better" is not coaching. "At 2:14, when the client said 'how much,' you answered the question instead of holding the frame — next time, redirect with 'Let me build that number once I have your data'" — that's coaching.
3. **Everyone participates.** If you're in the room, you're diagnosing. You're not a spectator.
4. **The developing rep goes last.** Don't start with the weakest call — it kills the room. Start with the strong call. Build energy. Then diagnose.
5. **Corrections happen live.** The rep re-delivers the corrected version in the room. Not later. Now.
6. **Product knowledge gaps are separate.** Don't try to coach product knowledge and selling technique in the same correction. Flag it, assign the study, and review next session.

### Escalation Thresholds

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Same weakness for 3+ consecutive weeks | Intensive coaching. Daily practice assignment. Manager on calls. |
| Compliance issue (guarantee language, misrepresentation) | Same-day correction. Documented. Training on compliant positioning. |
| Zero transformation selling in premium-eligible conversations | The rep is leaving revenue and client value on the table. Dedicated training week. |
| Consistently below 90 on QA scorecard | Structured development plan. Timeline for improvement. |
| Elite performer plateau | Assign peer coaching role. Challenge with advanced scenarios. |
