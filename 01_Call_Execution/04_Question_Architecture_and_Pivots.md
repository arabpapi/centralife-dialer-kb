# Question Architecture and Pivots

## How This Works

- Every question has a job. If you don't know the job, don't ask it.
- Questions are organized in stacks. A stack is a sequence designed to uncover a specific layer of information.
- Pivot questions redirect without permission. You don't ask "can I ask you about" — you just transition.
- No filler. No "tell me about yourself." No "so what's going on?" Every question earns data or creates movement.

---

## Phase 1: Hypothesis Opener Architecture

### 1A — The Hypothesis Statement (Not a Question)
The first "question" isn't a question — it's a teaching statement.

**Structure:**
"The reason I'm reaching out — I work with [demographic match], and what we're consistently finding is [insight about their gap or structural misalignment]."

**Examples by profile:**

| Profile | Hypothesis |
|---------|-----------|
| Homeowner, 30s–40s, kids | "What we're finding is there's usually a 3–5x gap between what families carry and what the math actually requires — especially once you factor in the mortgage and income replacement." |
| Dual-income couple | "What we see with dual-income households is both incomes are load-bearing. The assumption that one can absorb the other's loss is almost always wrong once you run the numbers." |
| Self-employed | "Business owners are almost always over-leveraged and under-protected. The business doesn't generate income without you — and there's no employer benefit to fall back on." |
| Approaching retirement | "What we're seeing with clients in their 50s is three gaps: the tax efficiency of their retirement income, the estate transfer plan, and whether the coverage they've had for 20 years still matches their current situation." |

### 1B — The Credibility Frame
Immediately after the hypothesis, establish why you're credible.

"I've worked with over [X] families in this area. I shop 30+ carriers independently — I'm not pushing one company. I just want to run the numbers and see if there's something here worth addressing."

*No "if that's okay" — no "sound fair?" — no permission language. State it and move forward.*

### 1C — The Transition to Discovery
"Let me walk through your situation — it takes about 10 minutes — so I can see what we're working with and whether any of this applies."

This is not a request. It's a statement of what happens next.

## Phase 2: IPAD Discovery Stacks

### Stack 1 — Income Architecture

**Job:** Quantify the income that would need to be replaced.

| # | Question | Job |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | "What does your household take-home look like per month?" | Baseline replacement need |
| 2 | "Is that one income or two?" | Load-bearing analysis |
| 3 | "Which income drives the primary obligations — mortgage, debt, day-to-day?" | Identify critical income |
| 4 | "If that income stopped tomorrow, how many months does your current setup cover before the math breaks?" | Self-assessment of vulnerability |
| 5 | "What's the salary trajectory — stable, growing, variable?" | Future liability projection |

**Follow-up if dual income:** "If you lost one income entirely — not temporarily, permanently — what changes? What gets cut first?"

---

### Stack 2 — Protection Audit

**Job:** Surface the gap between current coverage and actual need.

| # | Question | Job |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | "What does your current life insurance look like — group through work, personal, or both?" | Coverage inventory |
| 2 | "What's the face amount?" | Quantify existing coverage |
| 3 | "Is the employer coverage portable — does it follow you if you leave?" | Expose portability risk |
| 4 | "When was the personal policy written? Has it been reviewed since?" | Surface outdated math |
| 5 | "Does the policy have any riders — living benefits, waiver of premium, accelerated death benefit?" | Assess structural completeness |

**Pivot out:** "I'm seeing $[X] in coverage against a need that's probably $[Y] based on what you've told me. Let me confirm that with a few more questions."

---

### Stack 3 — Asset and Debt Mapping

**Job:** Build the complete liability picture.

| # | Question | Job |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | "Walk me through the major debts — mortgage balance, auto loans, student loans, credit lines." | Total debt liability |
| 2 | "What's the monthly obligation across all of that?" | Monthly burn rate |
| 3 | "What does the savings picture look like — liquid savings, emergency fund?" | Liquidity buffer |
| 4 | "How many months of current expenses would that cover if income stopped?" | Burn rate timeline |
| 5 | "Any retirement accounts — 401k, IRA, pension?" | Retirement exposure |
| 6 | "What's the approximate balance? Are you contributing to it currently?" | Quantify retirement assets |
| 7 | "Any loans against the retirement accounts?" | Hidden liabilities |

**Pivot out:** "Here's where we are — income at $[X], debts at $[Y], savings covers about [Z] months. Let me pull the protection piece together."

---

### Stack 4 — Medical and Underwriting

**Job:** Carrier matching and rate class determination. Neutral delivery on every question.

| # | Question | Job |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | "Any tobacco use in the last 12 months?" | Tobacco rate class |
| 2 | "Current medications — anything prescribed regularly?" | Medication profile |
| 3 | "Any major health events — hospitalizations, surgeries, chronic conditions?" | Underwriting flags |
| 4 | "Family health history — parents, siblings — anything significant?" | Carrier requirements |
| 5 | "Any DUI, legal issues, or driving record concerns?" | Non-medical underwriting |
| 6 | "Have you ever been declined or rated for life insurance?" | Placement history |

**Handling disclosures:** "That's not uncommon. We have carriers built for that profile. Let me note it so I place you correctly." Then move on. Zero pause. Zero tone change.

## Phase 3: Deeper Discovery Stacks

### Stack 5 — Retirement and Tax Efficiency

**Job:** Uncover whether the client's retirement strategy has structural gaps insurance can address.

| # | Question | Job |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | "How are you positioning your retirement savings — all in one type of account, or diversified across tax treatments?" | Tax bucket analysis |
| 2 | "Is most of your retirement in pre-tax accounts — 401k, traditional IRA?" | Identify tax concentration risk |
| 3 | "Have you thought about what the tax rate looks like when you start withdrawing?" | Tax-awareness level |
| 4 | "What happens to Social Security taxation if your retirement income pushes you into a higher bracket?" | Social Security exposure |
| 5 | "Do you have any assets you can access tax-free in retirement — Roth, policy loans, anything?" | Identify the gap for tax-free income |

**Pivot out:** "Most people have all their retirement eggs in one tax basket. That's not a crisis — it's a planning opportunity. Let me show you what adding a tax-free access layer looks like."

---

### Stack 6 — Legacy and Generational Planning

**Job:** Surface whether the client has thought about what they leave behind and how efficiently it transfers.

| # | Question | Job |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | "What's your current estate plan? Will, trust, or nothing formal?" | Estate planning status |
| 2 | "If something happened to both of you, who raises the kids and with what resources?" | Guardianship + funding |
| 3 | "Do you want to leave the next generation in a better financial position than you started?" | Legacy intent |
| 4 | "Have you thought about how your assets transfer — probate, tax implications, timing?" | Transfer efficiency awareness |
| 5 | "Is there a family business or real estate that needs liquidity at death for succession or equalization?" | Business/estate liquidity need |

**Pivot out:** "Here's the thing — a death benefit creates immediate, income-tax-free liquidity. Depending on how it's structured, it can bypass probate, fund a trust, equalize an inheritance, or cover estate obligations. Let me model what that looks like for your situation."

---

### Stack 7 — Future-Self and Transformation

**Job:** Move the conversation from current risk to future opportunity.

| # | Question | Job |
|---|----------|-----|
| 1 | "Where do you want to be financially in 10 years — income, net worth, lifestyle?" | Future-state anchor |
| 2 | "What's the biggest financial risk between now and then?" | Self-identified vulnerability |
| 3 | "If one major health event happened between now and retirement, what breaks?" | Living benefit relevance |
| 4 | "What does financial independence look like for your family — not just for you?" | Expand the stakeholder frame |
| 5 | "If you could build a financial structure today that protects, accumulates, and transfers — would that change how you think about this?" | Close the conceptual gap |

**Pivot out:** "That's exactly what proper financial architecture does — it hedges today's risk while building future value. Let me show you what that looks like with your numbers."

## Phase 4: Pivot Questions (Transitions Between Stages)

### Discovery → Rational Drowning
"Here's what I'm seeing based on everything you've told me —" then state the gap.

### Rational Drowning → Emotional Impact
"And here's what that means in real terms for [spouse name] and [children names] —"

### Emotional Impact → New Way
"The right financial architecture closes that gap and does three more things —"

### New Way → Transformation Bridge
"That's the protection piece. Here's the other side — what this does for you while you're alive."

### Transformation Bridge → Option Presentation
"Let me show you two paths. Both solve the core problem. One does more."

### Option Presentation → Application
"Let's lock this in while your rate is at this level. I need a few details to get started."

### Application → Post-Close
"This is done. Two things before we go —"

## Phase 5: Diagnostic Questions for Specific Moments

### When the client claims they're "all set"
- "When was the last time someone ran the actual number against your current obligations?"
- "Has your income, mortgage, or family picture changed since that policy was written?"
- "Does your current coverage include living benefits — chronic, critical, terminal?"

### When the client deflects to a third party
- "What specifically would your spouse/advisor want to know? I'll make sure we cover it."
- "Is there a concern they have that I can address now so you're both working from the same data?"

### When the client gets emotional
*Slow down. Lower your voice. Use their names.*
- "This is exactly why we're having this conversation — so you're not making these decisions under pressure later."
- "Your family's security is worth 20 minutes. Let's build something that holds."

### When the client asks for a price before discovery
- "I'll build that number for you — but I need your actual data to make it accurate. A generic quote doesn't tell you anything useful."
- "The cost depends on your health class, your age, and how much we're solving for. Let me get those details and I'll give you a specific number, not a range."

---

## Rules

1. Every question has a stated job. No exceptions.
2. No filler questions: "So tell me a little about yourself" — eliminated.
3. No permission questions: "Would it be okay if I asked" — eliminated.
4. Follow-ups go 3 levels deep minimum on critical data points.
5. Always state the number you uncovered before moving to the next stack.
6. Pivots are statements, not requests.
